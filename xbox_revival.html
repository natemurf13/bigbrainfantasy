Instructions:

  1. Take apart Xbox and place motherboard on heater
  2. Set bottom heater temp to 100 degrees celsius and wait 10 minutes
  3. Turn on top heater for CPU and set to 90 degrees celsius and wait 15 minutes
  4. Move top heater to GPU and set to 90 degrees celsius and wait 15 minutes
  5. Remove top heater and wait 5 minutes
  6. Turn bottom heater off and wait 2 hours
  7. Apply fresh thermal paste and re attach heat sinks
  8. Start it up and make sure it works good as new!

The RROD, which is short for "Red Ring of Death" happens when the solder connections underneath the CPU or GPU crack and are no longer making good contact between the board and the chip. This is due to the fact that lead free solder is used on this board, because it is much cheaper. Normally this would be a fine substitute, but with the poor air flow design of the xbox 360, it can get hot enough to start to melt the solder a bit, and then the solder will re solidify, and over time this causes cracks, giving players the RROD.

Luckily, there is a quick fix for this to get an extra few months or so out of your console. You will need a reflow station as the above picture shows, that will evenly heat up the bottom of the board, and a heat gun to heat the chips from above.
